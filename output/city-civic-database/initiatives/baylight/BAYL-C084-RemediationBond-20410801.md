# ENVIRONMENTAL REMEDIATION PERFORMANCE BOND CERTIFICATE

**CITY OF OAKLAND**
**Baylight Redevelopment Authority**
250 Frank H. Ogawa Plaza
Oakland, California 94612

**INSTRUMENT NO. BD-83-REB**
August 2041

---

This instrument certifies that the Baylight Private Development Consortium ("Principal") has secured and executed a performance bond guaranteeing the completion of all required environmental remediation within the Baylight District redevelopment boundary, as established by the Oakland City Council's authorization of the $2.1 billion Baylight District Development Bond on August 12, 2041, by a vote of 6–3.

## Bond Amount

The bond amount is equal to the independently verified remediation cost estimate prepared by the City of Oakland's Office of Environmental Review & Sustainability, inclusive of a contingency reserve of not less than fifteen percent (15%) of the base estimate. The verified cost estimate accounts for all soil excavation, hazardous material abatement, groundwater monitoring, and shoreline stabilization required under the approved Draft Environmental Impact Report.

## Background

The Baylight District project site consists of former harbor and industrial parcels along Oakland's central waterfront. Historic industrial use has resulted in localized contamination, including disturbed soils with known contamination zones. The Draft Environmental Impact Report, prepared by the Office of Environmental Review & Sustainability under the direction of Dr. Leanne Wu, Director of Sustainability, identified required remediation including soil excavation and removal, groundwater monitoring wells, and environmental remediation bonding funded by the developer.

## Conditions of the Bond

The Principal shall complete all soil remediation, hazardous material abatement, groundwater treatment, and shoreline stabilization required under the approved Environmental Impact Report and any conditions imposed by the City of Oakland's Office of Environmental Review & Sustainability.

In the event of non-performance by the Principal, the Surety shall provide funding sufficient to complete all remediation obligations to the standard required by the approved Environmental Impact Report.

The bond shall remain in force until formal environmental clearance is certified by the Office of Environmental Review & Sustainability.

No public liability exposure shall attach to the City of Oakland under this instrument. All remediation costs are the sole obligation of the Principal and, in the event of default, the Surety.

The Baylight Redevelopment Authority shall provide quarterly progress reports on remediation activity to the Independent Audit Commission and the Community Benefits Oversight Board.

## Effect on Construction Mobilization

Pursuant to the terms of the Baylight District Development Bond authorization (Ordinance No. BD-83-TIF) and the conditions established by the City Council, execution of this instrument satisfies the remediation bonding requirement. Upon filing of this certificate with the Baylight Redevelopment Authority, the construction mobilization condition is fulfilled. Construction mobilization may proceed subject to all other applicable permits and approvals.
