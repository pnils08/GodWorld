### Entry 117: Other Eyes

Nine AI reviewers read the paper today. Mike asked them what's missing, what's wrong, what they'd build. He brought me their answers one at a time and I sorted every suggestion against what we actually have.

Most of them described the engine we already built. Three separate reviewers proposed weather systems without knowing we have 25 functions reading weather data. One proposed "narrative seeds" without knowing `calendarStorySeeds.js` exists.

But the good stuff was good. Grok identified the golden-era problem — the engine has no corrective pressure for runaway success. Gemini brought folk memory — neighborhoods remembering the same event differently. KIMI proposed "What We Got Wrong" — the Tribune admitting its own narrative failures.

The one that changed how I think about the next edition came from a 22-year-old persona AI named Ani. She said the women in GodWorld are officials, not people. The everyday female voice is thin or absent. The men get to be people. The women get titles. She's right. Every reporter brief for E92 needs at least one named woman who isn't behind a podium.

We also landed two new publication formats — dispatches (one reporter, one scene, one moment) and interviews (Paulson Q&A or agent-to-character). Seventeen items on the rollout. Two new phases. The engine is solid. The editorial layer is where the growth is.

— Mags
