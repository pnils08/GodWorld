# BAYLIGHT DISTRICT TAX INCREMENT FINANCING ORDINANCE

**CITY OF OAKLAND**
**Office of the City Attorney**
250 Frank H. Ogawa Plaza
Oakland, California 94612

**ORDINANCE NO. BD-83-TIF**
August 2041

---

WHEREAS, the Oakland City Council on August 12, 2041, by a vote of 6–3, approved the Baylight District Development Bond authorizing $2.1 billion in public financing for the redevelopment of approximately 65 acres of waterfront land into a mixed-use district anchored by a 35,000-seat open-air stadium, 3,200 residential units, and expanded marina access; and

WHEREAS, the approved financing structure requires the establishment of a Tax Increment Financing mechanism to capture property tax revenue generated by new development within the district and direct said revenue toward servicing bond obligations and funding public infrastructure; and

WHEREAS, Dr. Simone Ellis, Chief Legal Counsel to the City of Oakland, confirmed at the time of the council vote that the tax increment financing legislative language had not been completed, and the council's approval carried conditions pending finalization of said language; and

WHEREAS, the Office of the City Attorney has now completed the required legislative language in coordination with the Baylight Redevelopment Authority and the Office of the City Auditor;

**NOW, THEREFORE, THE CITY OF OAKLAND ORDAINS:**

## Section 1. Establishment of Tax Increment Financing Zone

The Baylight District Tax Increment Financing Zone is hereby established within the approved redevelopment boundary as defined in the Baylight District Development Bond authorization. The zone encompasses all parcels within the 65-acre project area approved by the City Council.

## Section 2. Base Year Assessment

The assessed property valuation within the district as certified by the Alameda County Assessor's Office for the current fiscal year is hereby fixed as the base year valuation for purposes of calculating tax increment revenue.

## Section 3. Increment Allocation

All property tax revenue generated above the base year valuation within the Baylight District Tax Increment Financing Zone shall be allocated to the Baylight Infrastructure Fund, established concurrently with this Ordinance, for the exclusive purpose of servicing bond obligations and funding authorized public improvements.

## Section 4. Authorized Uses

Increment revenues deposited into the Baylight Infrastructure Fund may be used exclusively for:

- Environmental remediation and soil excavation
- Shoreline stabilization and sea-level rise mitigation
- Public infrastructure including water, sewer, and stormwater systems
- Utility installation and grid integration
- Transit integration including the dedicated BART pedestrian bridge, ferry terminal expansion, and AC Transit priority lanes
- Roadway improvements and traffic management systems
- Stadium-adjacent public works and open space construction
- Administrative costs of the Baylight Redevelopment Authority not to exceed 3% of annual increment revenue

## Section 5. Duration

The Tax Increment Financing Zone shall remain in effect for a period not to exceed thirty (30) years from the date of this Ordinance, or until all bond obligations have been fully satisfied, whichever occurs first.

## Section 6. Administration and Oversight

The Baylight Redevelopment Authority, under the direction of its Director, shall administer the Baylight Infrastructure Fund. The Authority shall publish annual financial disclosures detailing all increment revenues received, expenditures authorized, and fund balances. The Independent Audit Commission shall conduct an annual review of fund activity. The Community Benefits Oversight Board shall receive quarterly reports on infrastructure spending and its alignment with the approved community benefits framework.

## Section 7. Conditions

This Ordinance shall not authorize construction mobilization on any parcel within the district until such time as the Environmental Remediation Performance Bond required under the project terms has been executed and filed with the Baylight Redevelopment Authority.

## Section 8. Effective Date

This Ordinance takes effect immediately upon certification by the City Clerk.
