**Workforce Development Initiative**  
*Status: Program Design Phase — Launch Targeted Year 3*

-----

## I. Program Overview

The Oakland Youth Apprenticeship Pipeline is a structured workforce development program connecting Oakland high school students and young adults (ages 16-24) to careers in construction trades, with direct placement pathways into Baylight District and other major development projects.

The program converts the Baylight District’s 45% local hire commitment from a contract provision into a measurable pipeline — training Oakland residents before shovels hit ground so the jobs go to people who are ready.

**Lead Agency:** Oakland Workforce Development Board  
**Partner Agencies:** OUSD, Peralta Community College District, Building Trades Council  
**Target Launch:** Year 3, Q1 (18 months before Baylight construction)

## III. Program Structure

### Track 1: High School Pathway (Ages 16-18)

- **Entry Point:** Junior year at participating OUSD schools
- **Format:** After-school and summer intensive
- **Duration:** 18-24 months
- **Outcome:** Pre-apprenticeship certificate, OSHA-10, industry credentials

**Participating Schools (Initial):**

- Oakland Technical High School
- Skyline High School
- Fremont High School
- Castlemont High School
- McClymonds High School

### Track 2: Young Adult Pathway (Ages 18-24)

- **Entry Point:** Open enrollment, quarterly cohorts
- **Format:** Full-time (32 hrs/week) paid training
- **Duration:** 12 months
- **Outcome:** Registered apprenticeship placement, union referral

### Track 3: Direct Placement (Ages 18+)

- **Entry Point:** Graduates of Track 1 or 2, or equivalent credentials
- **Format:** Job matching with Baylight contractors
- **Duration:** Ongoing
- **Outcome:** Employment at prevailing wage

## VII. Connection to Baylight District

The Baylight Community Benefits Agreement includes:

- **45% Oakland resident hire** for construction phase
- **First-source hiring agreement** — Baylight contractors must post openings with Oakland Workforce Development Board before external recruitment
- **Apprenticeship utilization requirement** — Minimum 20% of labor hours performed by registered apprentices

The Pipeline is the supply-side answer. Without it, the 45% mandate becomes a compliance problem — contractors scrambling to find qualified local workers at construction start. With it, Oakland residents are trained, credentialed, and first in line.

**Projected Baylight Construction Jobs:** 8,500 over 30 months  
**45% Local Hire Target:** 3,825 positions  
**Pipeline Contribution (Year 3+):** 400-600 direct placements annually

**Opposition/Concerns:**

**Low Political Risk:** This is the feel-good initiative. Hard to oppose training kids for good-paying jobs.

## X. Program Narrative

Oakland has promised itself Baylight will be different. Not a stadium that gets built by workers bused in from Stockton. Not a development that locals can only experience as customers.

The Youth Apprenticeship Pipeline is the mechanism. A 17-year-old at Oakland Tech today could be a second-year electrical apprentice pulling wire at the stadium site by Year 4. That’s not abstract benefit — that’s a career with a pension and a union card.

The 45% local hire commitment means nothing if Oakland doesn’t have 45% ready to work. The Pipeline makes sure they are.
