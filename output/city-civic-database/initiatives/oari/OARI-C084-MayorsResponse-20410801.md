# OAKLAND ALTERNATIVE RESPONSE INITIATIVE — Implementation Status

**OFFICE OF THE MAYOR**
**CITY OF OAKLAND**

Response to Planning Office Inquiry
August 2041

---

**TO:** Mara Vance, City Planning Director
**FROM:** Laila Cortez, Chief of Staff, Office of the Mayor

---

Director Vance,

Thank you for the status request. The Mayor's office appreciates the Planning Office's diligence in tracking implementation timelines, and we share your commitment to meeting the obligations this initiative establishes.

The Oakland Alternative Response Initiative represents one of the most significant investments this city has made in behavioral health infrastructure. The council voted to fund it. The Mayor signed it. The 45-day window for pilot district selection is running, and this office intends to meet that deadline — not because of external pressure, but because the people who need this program cannot wait for bureaucratic delay.

What follows is a comprehensive update on implementation status across all areas your office identified.

## I. Pilot District Selection

The Mayor's office has completed its evaluation and is prepared to announce pilot district selections. The three districts selected are:

- District 1 (West Oakland)
- District 3 (Fruitvale)
- District 5

Selection criteria included: non-violent crisis call volume per capita, existing community health infrastructure, council member cooperation, geographic distribution across the city, and operational feasibility for initial deployment.

The Mayor's office consulted with the council members representing each selected district. No council members representing non-selected districts were consulted on the final selection, though all nine offices were briefed on the initiative's implementation framework following the vote.

Public announcement of the pilot districts will be made by the Mayor's office within the coming days.

## II. Hiring Criteria and Timeline

### Team Composition

Each response team consists of two members: one licensed behavioral health clinician (LCSW, LMFT, or licensed psychologist) and one certified peer counselor with lived experience in behavioral health recovery, homelessness, or substance use. Teams will be deployed in marked vehicles with medical assessment kits, de-escalation equipment, and direct communication links to OPD dispatch for safety coordination.

### Staffing Targets

- Initial deployment: three teams per pilot district, nine teams total
- Full deployment target: five teams per district, fifteen teams total, by end of first evaluation period
- Total personnel at initial deployment: 18 (9 clinicians, 9 peer counselors)
- Total personnel at full deployment: 30 (15 clinicians, 15 peer counselors)

### Recruitment Timeline

- Job descriptions finalized and approved by Human Resources Division
- Public posting projected within one week of pilot district announcement
- Application window: 21 days from posting date
- Screening and interviews: 14 days following application close
- Background checks and credentialing: concurrent with interview process
- Conditional offers to first cohort: projected approximately six weeks after posting

### Training Program

- Duration: 80 hours (two weeks full-time equivalent)
- Curriculum developed by Department of Violence Prevention with consultation from Alameda County Behavioral Health
- Modules include: crisis de-escalation, field assessment protocols, OPD dispatch coordination procedures, cultural competency, mandatory reporting obligations, and scenario-based field exercises
- Training facility: Department of Violence Prevention headquarters

## III. Facility and Vehicle Staging

- District 1 staging will use existing Department of Violence Prevention facilities in West Oakland
- District 5 and District 3 require new staging arrangements — Planning Office requested to coordinate site identification and lease negotiation
- Nine response vehicles approved for procurement (one per team at initial deployment)
- Projected vehicle delivery: four to six weeks from order confirmation
- Medical assessment kits and communications equipment sourced through existing vendor relationships

## IV. Community Advisory Board

Composition:

- Two representatives from pilot district communities
- One licensed behavioral health professional
- One representative from the Department of Violence Prevention
- One representative nominated by the council
- One representative from Oakland's homeless services network
- One representative with lived experience in behavioral health system

Total membership: seven. Board seated within 30 days of pilot district announcement. Meeting frequency: monthly during initial deployment, quarterly thereafter.

The board is advisory. Operational decisions remain with the Department of Violence Prevention under the Mayor's authority.

## V. Public Communication

The Mayor's office will hold a public announcement event for the pilot district selections, including:

- Mayor Santana's remarks on the initiative's purpose and the selection process
- Councilmembers Carter, Rivers, and Delgado in attendance as district representatives
- Department of Violence Prevention director presenting the deployment framework
- Open Q&A with credentialed press

Theo Park is the point of contact for press inquiries during the implementation period.

## VI. The Timeline

Director Vance, your office asked where this initiative stands. Here is where it stands: ahead of schedule.

The 45-day window has not expired. Pilot districts are selected. Hiring criteria are developed. The advisory board structure is designed. Staging coordination is underway.

This office is aware that the initiative passed 5–4 and that organized opposition ran a targeted campaign against it. We are also aware that Dante Nelson, a security guard in Downtown, asked a reasonable question after the vote: what does this mean for my block? The answer is: it means three districts get pilot deployment now, and the data from those pilots will determine whether and how the program expands to the rest of the city.

Downtown is not in the first round. The call volume data and community infrastructure assessment did not place it among the top three. When the pilot data supports expansion, Downtown will be among the first districts evaluated for the next phase.

We will meet the timeline.
