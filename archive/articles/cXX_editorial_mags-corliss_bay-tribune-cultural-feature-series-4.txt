Born in California to Japanese-American parents, Ka has become one of the most quietly influential figures in Oakland’s modern story — a pitcher who turned every inning into a cultural bridge.  His name might appear in the box score next to earned run averages and strikeout totals, but what’s happening behind those numbers reaches far beyond the foul lines.
