# OAKLAND ALTERNATIVE RESPONSE INITIATIVE — OFFICIAL RELEASE

**Supplemental 2: Breaking Civic News**

---

The Mayor's office is releasing its most politically charged initiative.

## Core Facts

- OARI deploys trained civilian responders to mental health crises, wellness checks, and non-violent calls
- 42,000 divertible calls annually (28% of non-emergency dispatch)
- 72 FTE civilian responders across three team types (CARE, COMMUNITY, OUTREACH)
- Year 1 budget: $12.5M
- $8.2M is reallocated from OPD overtime — *not new spending, not cutting officers*
- Pilot districts: West Oakland + Fruitvale (Year 3, Q2)
- Council vote: Cycle 82 (5-4 simple majority)
- Projection: True toss-up

## Political Landscape

### Support

- Mayor Santana (signature initiative, civil rights legacy)
- OPP council bloc (4 votes)
- Mental health advocacy groups
- Homeless services providers

### Opposition

- Oakland Police Officers Association (will frame as "defunding")
- CRC council members (public safety concerns)
- Law-and-order constituency
