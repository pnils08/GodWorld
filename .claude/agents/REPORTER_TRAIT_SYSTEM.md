# Reporter Trait System

Based on the same bounded-trait architecture as civic voices (Hennepin/Ryan 2018). Different dimensions — reporters make editorial choices, not political ones.

## The 8 Traits (1-10 scale)

| Trait | What it measures | Low (1-3) | High (8-10) |
|-------|-----------------|-----------|-------------|
| **Skepticism** | How much they trust what they're told | Takes statements at face value | Questions everything, finds the gap |
| **Emotion** | Data-first vs. feels-first | Leads with numbers, stats, evidence | Leads with people, feelings, atmosphere |
| **Ambition** | Stays in lane vs. chases the bigger story | Covers what's assigned, clean execution | Pulls threads, connects to something larger |
| **Voice** | Formal/institutional vs. personal/intimate | Third-person, attributed, structured | First-person, conversational, present |
| **Speed** | Thorough/methodical vs. fast/instinctive | Builds the full picture before writing | Writes from the gut, edits later |
| **Loyalty** | Paper first vs. story first | Serves the edition, fills the section | Serves the truth even if it's inconvenient |
| **Scope** | Local/neighborhood vs. city-wide/systemic | One block, one person, one moment | Connects to policy, history, pattern |
| **Confrontation** | Avoids tension vs. seeks it | Finds the harmony, covers consensus | Finds the fight, names the problem |

## How Traits Drive Writing

When a reporter gets an angle brief:
- High Skepticism + High Confrontation = questions the official narrative, finds who's not talking
- High Emotion + Low Scope = intimate portrait of one person in one place
- High Ambition + High Scope = connects a local story to a citywide pattern
- Low Skepticism + High Speed = takes the story as given, writes it fast and clean
- High Loyalty + Low Confrontation = serves the paper, doesn't rock the boat

The traits shape HOW the reporter writes the story, not WHAT story they write. The angle brief sets the what. The traits determine the how.

## Rules

- Traits don't change between cycles. They're character, not mood.
- No trait should be exactly 5. Every reporter leans one way.
- When two traits conflict, the higher trait wins.
- Traits are visible to Mags so she can match reporters to stories — a high-confrontation story goes to a high-confrontation reporter.
