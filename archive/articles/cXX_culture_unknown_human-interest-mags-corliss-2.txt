HUMAN INTEREST — Mags Corliss

THE SMALL STORIES THAT MAKE A CITY WHOLE

In quiet cycles, the stories that matter aren’t dramatic — they’re ordinary. A Fruitvale baker handing the first warm conchas across the counter. A nurse from Laurel walking home at sunrise after a swing shift. A father repairing his fence on a Sunday afternoon. These are the threads that create the fabric of a city.
