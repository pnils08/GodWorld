## I. Project Overview

The Temescal Community Health Center is a proposed 42,000 sq ft neighborhood health facility designed to provide primary care, urgent care, mental health services, and public health response capacity to the Temescal district and surrounding neighborhoods.

The proposal emerges from the ongoing Wave 4 health crisis — but the facility is designed for permanence, not emergency response. Whether or not the current outbreak is resolved, Temescal lacks adequate healthcare infrastructure for its population density.

**Proposed Location:** Telegraph Avenue corridor (Temescal)  
**Target Opening:** Year 3, Q4  
**Lead Agency:** Oakland Department of Health & Human Services

## III. Facility Components

### Clinical Services

- **Primary Care Clinic:** 12 exam rooms, 4 providers on staff
- **Urgent Care Wing:** 8 treatment bays, extended hours (7am-11pm)
- **Behavioral Health Suite:** 6 therapy rooms, psychiatric services, crisis stabilization
- **Pediatric Clinic:** Dedicated 4-room children’s health unit
- **Women’s Health:** OB/GYN services, prenatal care, family planning

### Public Health Infrastructure

- **Disease Surveillance Lab:** Sample collection and preliminary testing capacity
- **Community Health Education:** 80-seat classroom/meeting space
- **Mobile Unit Garage:** Housing for 2 mobile health vans serving adjacent neighborhoods
- **Emergency Operations Room:** Convertible space for outbreak response coordination

### Support Services

- **Pharmacy:** On-site dispensary (generic/essential medications)
- **Social Services Office:** Benefits enrollment, housing assistance referrals
- **Community Kitchen:** Nutrition education, WIC distribution point

## VI. Connection to Current Crisis

The Wave 4 health event in Temescal has exposed gaps in neighborhood health infrastructure:

- Nearest urgent care is 2.1 miles from the district center
- No dedicated behavioral health services within the neighborhood
- Community clinics at capacity; 3+ week waits for new patient appointments
- No local disease surveillance capability — samples must be transported to County

The Community Health Center does not solve the current outbreak. But it ensures the next health event — whatever form it takes — meets an institutional response rather than improvised mutual aid.

## IX. Project Narrative

Temescal has been waiting for something the city couldn’t name. Neighbors organized meal trains while clinics posted three-week wait times. Mutual aid networks filled gaps that should have been filled by institutions.

The Community Health Center is the institution. Not a response to Wave 4 specifically — the city still hasn’t explained Wave 4 — but a recognition that a neighborhood of this density cannot function without healthcare infrastructure that matches its population.

The debate isn’t whether Temescal needs this. The debate is whether the city prioritizes building it.
