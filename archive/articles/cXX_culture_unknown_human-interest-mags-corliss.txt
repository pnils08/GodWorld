HUMAN INTEREST — Mags Corliss

THE QUIET MOMENTS THAT HOLD A CITY TOGETHER

Oakland’s beauty shows up strongest in the unremarkable moments. A grandmother standing outside a bakery with her morning coffee. A father pushing a stroller down International Boulevard. A nurse stepping out of Highland Hospital after a long shift, tying her hair back as she looks for her rideshare.

Days like these don’t demand headlines. They ask only to be noticed. When the city runs without a ripple, people rediscover the smaller parts of life that get drowned out by noise the rest of the year.
