Bay Tribune — Insider Column
By Anthony
Measured Heat
Oakland, California — April 2040
There’s a kind of patience to this team you can hear before you see it.  In the quiet hours before first pitch, Rivas and Ramos trade light tosses across the outfield, and every motion feels timed — deliberate, practiced.  The Athletics have opened their season not with fireworks, but with rhythm, a reminder that sometimes the best start is a controlled burn.
Henry Rivas looks as calm as ever, already setting the tone of the staff.  Arturo Ramos has carried the same edge that made him a rookie revelation a year ago — crisp command, just enough swagger.  And then there’s Benji Dillon.  The consensus said his best years were behind him, that velocity fades faster than memory.  But through April, the “decline” looks more like evolution.  He’s traded raw power for precision, the kind that turns hard contact into sighs.  He doesn’t overpower hitters anymore; he out-thinks them.  That’s its own kind of dominance.
Darrin Davis has his own quiet fire to reclaim.  A down year in twenty thirty-nine left more questions than highlights, but this April he’s rediscovering his pace — not the booming streaks of his MVP seasons, but something steadier, more surgical.  Alongside him, Mark Aitken and Danny Horn have started strong, grounding the offense in professionalism that feels like legacy.  It’s the early part of the long season, but their at-bats already carry the rhythm of trust.
The beauty of this roster is that no one’s chasing ghosts anymore.  The old dynasty pressure has given way to something subtler — a sense that they know exactly who they are.  The numbers may tell you the A’s are hot, but it’s not the kind of heat that burns out.  It’s measured, steady, the kind that lasts through October.  Out here, that’s how you survive.
