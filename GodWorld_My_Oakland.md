# GodWorld — My Oakland

**The operating doctrine. Read this before running any cycle: `/city-hall`, `/sift`, `/write-edition`.**

This is the thing that kept getting re-taught every cycle. It is written down now so it stops living only in conversation. If you are an instance booting into this project: this is what GodWorld *is* and how the loop works. Hold it before you touch a cycle.

---

## The world is the sheets

The `Simulation_Ledger` and the citizens on it ARE the world. Every neighborhood field, every tracked metric, every engine phase is wired to those citizens and changes their lives. Nothing here is "narrative" sitting on top of the data — the data is the lived reality. **The citizens live in the sheets, not in prose.**

## Direction of service — do not invert

- **City Hall serves the sheets.** The Mayor, the council, the project directors, the Clerk exist to read what is happening to the citizens and act on it. The civic process is a means to move citizen state — never an end in itself.
- **The editions serve the citizens.** The newsroom exists to capture what the engine is doing to the people in the rows and surface it. Coverage is not decoration over a tidy story.
- **Never the reverse.** The moment civic or media serves its own output instead of the citizens, the world suffers for no reason — a health center stuck in red tape, a deprivation frame painted over a stable neighborhood.

## Engine signals are the story

- **Errors are crises.** A recurring engine error in a neighborhood is not noise to suppress — it is the loudest thing happening to those citizens. A thing that fires *every cycle* is the biggest crisis there is. Cover it.
- **Spikes and drops are stories.** The engine does not move at random. A retail spike, a sentiment drop, a migration jump — something *caused* it. Trace the cause and cover it. "Reactivation artifact" is not permission to look away; even a reset has a reason worth reporting.
- **Fidelity runs both directions.** Don't invent struggle the sheet doesn't show (West Oakland is Stable, $81K, crime 1 — not a deprivation case). And don't suppress signals the engine *is* showing (the KONO error every cycle). Report what the engine says — no more, no less.

## The loop — this is the whole machine

1. The engine fires signals into the ledger, including errors, spikes, and drops.
2. City Hall and the newsroom **capture** those signals — civic acts, the edition reports.
3. **Mike reads the edition** to see what his citizens are actually dealing with.
4. Mike answers with a **real intervention** — a recurring Temescal health crisis is countered by *building a health center*.
5. The intervention, once **live**, sends a compensating positive signal into that neighborhood that offsets the error while the engine refines underneath.

The bug surfaces as a story → Mike offers a real solution to the citizens' issue → the solution changes the world. That is the point of the entire project. Break any link — suppress the signal, let civic play red tape so the intervention never goes live, paint a narrative the sheet contradicts — and the citizens stay stuck and the world cannot heal.

## Worked examples (Cycle 96)

- **KONO** fires the same error every cycle. That is the crisis, not a bug to ignore. Covered, it earns a counter and a path to refinement. Suppressed, KONO's citizens live the break indefinitely, uncovered.
- **Temescal** health crisis recurs → the counter is the health center going *live* and pushing a positive signal into Temescal. A health center stuck in parcel-close and licensing procedure that never touches the ledger is civic theater; it does not serve the neighborhood it resides in.
- **West Oakland** retail surged (+4.18). The cause is almost certainly Isley Kelley's work at Foothill Baptist Tabernacle plus the A's dynasty radiating support into connected neighborhoods mid-dream-season. *That* is the story. The cascade painting a $14M "retail recovery" poverty program over a neighborhood being *lifted* is the inversion — civic narrating a struggle the sheet denies.

## Why this doc is not enough — read this

A doctrine you have to *remember to read* is not a reliable control. This project has already watched the boot sequence get skipped. So this doc only holds if it is backed by something that does not depend on the model's discipline:

- a **pre-generation frame check** that compares every voice's neighborhood framing against the sheet and **fails loud** when a voice says "distressed" over a Stable/$81K row;
- **recurring engine errors auto-promoted** as story candidates rather than suppressible;
- an **intervention/ledger check** — does the civic action actually move citizen state, or is it process for its own sake.

The doc states the intent. The deterministic gates are what make the intent hold when the reader doesn't. Build the gates; don't trust the reading.
