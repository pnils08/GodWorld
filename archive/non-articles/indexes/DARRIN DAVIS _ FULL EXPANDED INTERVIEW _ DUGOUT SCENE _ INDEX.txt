Interview Feature (Expanded)

P Slayer: Ohio kid turns into the Outlaw of Oakland. That nickname doesn’t come from nowhere. What’s the story?
Darrin Davis: I wasn’t trying to sound tough. Oakland gave me that name before I earned it. I played like I was late for something. Full speed, no brake pedal. Sometimes I’d slide into a base so hard the dust would follow me into the next inning. People think outlaw means reckless. To me, it means ungoverned—in the good way.

P Slayer: Your game looks like chaos until it suddenly isn’t.
Darrin: That’s the trick. My whole life, coaches tried to sand down my edges. But the edges are the point. The speed, the instinct, the angles only work when I let them off the leash. Chaos scares people because it moves without asking first. I make it ask me.

P Slayer: You play center, second, anywhere they put you. You don’t even blink.
Darrin: A baseball field is a map. You learn the landmarks. You learn the pressure points. Once you get that, switching positions is just switching dialects of the same language. The ball talks. You listen.

P Slayer: Offensively, you’ve always had that spark plug vibe.
Darrin: Baseball needs sparks. Some guys are anchors, some are engines. I’m the wire that jumps the battery. I get on base, I turn the crowd up, I change the tempo. Not everything has to be pretty. Sometimes it just has to work.

P Slayer: Fans love your fearlessness. What fuels it?
Darrin: Simple. You get one career. One. I’m not saving any for later. I’m spending it all right now.
