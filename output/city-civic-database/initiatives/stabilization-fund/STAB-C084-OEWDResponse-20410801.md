# WEST OAKLAND EMERGENCY STABILIZATION FUND — Disbursement Status

**OFFICE OF ECONOMIC AND WORKFORCE DEVELOPMENT**
**CITY OF OAKLAND**

Response to Planning Office Inquiry
August 2041

---

**TO:** Mara Vance, City Planning Director
**FROM:** Sandra Liu, Deputy Director, Community Investment, OEWD

---

This office is responding to the Planning Office's status request regarding the West Oakland Emergency Stabilization Fund. We appreciate the inquiry and recognize that the timeline between authorization and disbursement has exceeded what applicants and the public were led to expect.

## I. Application Overview

- Total applications received: 342
- Original projection: 150–200 applications
- Applications approved and verified: 47
- Applications pending additional documentation: 110
- Applications unreviewed: 154
- Applications denied: 31

## II. What Caused the Delay

The application volume significantly exceeded initial projections. OEWD staffed the fund's intake process based on an estimate of approximately 150 to 200 applications. We received 342. The volume reflects the depth of need in West Oakland, which this office should have anticipated more accurately.

The original review team consisted of three staff members. In the fourth week of processing, it became clear that the team could not complete reviews at a pace consistent with responsible disbursement timelines. OEWD requested and received approval to bring on two additional review staff. Those staff have been active for approximately three weeks and have materially accelerated the review pace.

A secondary delay occurred in documentation verification. Approximately one-third of applications required follow-up for missing or incomplete documentation — proof of residency, income verification, business registration, or landlord confirmation. Applicants were notified by mail and given 14 days to respond.

None of these factors constitute an acceptable reason for applicants to have heard nothing since submitting their forms. This office should have issued a public status update when the volume exceeded projections and again when additional staff were onboarded. We did not. That is being corrected now.

## III. Disbursement Timeline

### First Round

- 47 applications approved and verified
- Total first-round disbursement: approximately $4.2 million
- Checks will be issued within three weeks of the date of this response
- All 47 approved applicants will receive individual notification by mail and phone prior to check issuance

### Second Round

- 110 applications currently pending additional documentation will be prioritized
- Second disbursement round projected approximately six weeks after the first round
- Estimated second-round volume: 50 to 70 additional approvals
- Estimated second-round disbursement: $5 to $7 million

### Ongoing Processing

- Remaining 154 unreviewed applications processed on a rolling basis through the fall
- Target: complete all initial reviews by end of October 2041
- Third and subsequent disbursement rounds will occur monthly until the $28 million allocation is fully committed

## IV. Review Criteria

### Eligibility Requirements

- Applicant must reside or operate a business within the West Oakland census tracts designated in the fund's authorizing resolution
- Residential applicants: household income at or below 80% of Area Median Income
- Business applicants: business must be currently operating in West Oakland with 10 or fewer FTE employees
- Applicant must demonstrate economic hardship tied to displacement pressure, rent escalation, or loss of income within the 12 months preceding application

### Prioritization Factors

- Businesses facing rent increases exceeding 15% year-over-year receive priority scoring
- Residential tenants who have received formal rent increase notices or eviction proceedings receive priority scoring
- Applicants with dependents under 18 or over 65 receive additional weight
- Length of West Oakland residency or business operation is a positive factor but not determinative

### Award Ranges

- Residential stabilization grants: $5,000 to $25,000
- Small business stabilization grants: $15,000 to $75,000
- Maximum single award: $75,000

## V. Public Communication Plan

Effective immediately:

- All approved applicants in the first round will receive individual notification by mail and phone at least 10 business days before check issuance
- All denied applicants will receive written explanation of the denial with instructions for appeal
- All applicants with pending documentation requests will receive a follow-up notice
- OEWD will post a public status update on its website
- Public point of contact for media inquiries: Sandra Liu, OEWD Deputy Director for Community Investment
- OEWD will provide quarterly status reports to the Office of City Planning and the Mayor's office

## VI. Acknowledgment

The Planning Office's status request noted that Darius Clark, a bakery worker in West Oakland, was handed an application form and told to wait. That characterization is accurate and it is not acceptable as a final answer.

Mr. Clark's experience represents approximately 342 similar experiences. Every one of those applicants submitted a form based on a council commitment, and every one of them has been operating since then without information about whether or when that commitment would be honored.

The first checks will go out within three weeks. The criteria are now public. The timeline is on the record.
