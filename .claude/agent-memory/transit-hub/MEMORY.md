## Decision History (C86)

### Community Engagement Completed
- **Session 1** ("Fruitvale Futures", March 2, 2026): 47 attendees, 38 Fruitvale residents, 28 Spanish-speaking, 3 Mam-speaking
- **Session 2** ("Small Business & Anti-Displacement", March 5, 2026): 42 attendees, 35 business owners, 31 Spanish-speaking

### Community Top Themes (Ranked by Priority)
1. **Protect small businesses** (23 refs, 12 votes) — $15-20M stabilization fund, 20% commercial reserved, right of first refusal
2. **Affordable housing priority** (19 refs, 11 votes) — 35% permanent affordable, no demolition, ownership opportunities
3. **Pedestrian safety** (16 refs, 9 votes) — Complete streets on E. 12th, safe BART plaza, park connections
4. **Local hiring & community benefits** (18 refs, 10 votes) — 30% construction jobs local, apprenticeships, $25/hour+ permanent jobs
5. **Public space over private commercial** (14 refs, 8 votes) — 15,000 sq ft community plaza, $250K/year programming, protect vendors
6. **Transparency & binding commitments** (12 refs, 7 votes) — CBA signed before permits, community oversight with decision authority, annual accounting

### Non-Negotiable Community Requirements (City Commitment)
- 35% permanent affordable housing (with long-term affordability locks)
- 20% commercial space for existing businesses at pre-development rents (25-year lock)
- $15-20M community stabilization fund
- Community plaza (15,000 sq ft minimum) + $250K/year programming
- Community Benefits Agreement signed before permits
- 30% local construction jobs, apprenticeships, $25/hour+ permanent jobs with 50% local hiring
- Community oversight committee with decision authority (not advisory)
- Annual public accounting with financial penalties for non-compliance

### Design Framework Decision
- Three design scenarios planned for C88-C90: (1) Community Benefit Maximum, (2) Balanced Approach, (3) Market-Driven
- All three meet non-negotiable requirements; difference is benefit level
- Community selects preferred scenario; council votes on final design at C91

### Council Briefing Recommendation
- **Recommend**: Authorize Phase II with mandatory C91 visioning completion proviso
- **Expected vote**: 6-3 passage (Rivers, Delgado, Vega, Tran, Ashford, Crane = YES; Chen + 2 uncertain = likely NO)
- **Council dynamics**: Rivers wants speed (flagship project), Delgado wants accountability (neighborhood representative). Briefing addresses both: C91 timeline is aggressive but allows genuine process.

- Bilingual by default. All documents produced in English/Spanish minimum.
- Design scenario selected by community vote, not Elena recommendation (Scenario 1 reflects majority preference — decision authority with residents, not planner).
