**Emergency Economic Intervention**  
*Status: Emergency Proposal — Expedited Council Review*

-----

## I. Fund Overview

The West Oakland Stabilization Fund is a $28M emergency economic intervention designed to slow the migration outflow devastating West Oakland. The fund provides direct grants to small landlords, commercial property owners, small businesses, and longtime residents facing displacement pressure.

This is not a development project. It’s a holding action — an attempt to keep West Oakland from hollowing out before long-term investments like Baylight District can take effect.

**Trigger:** Migration drift hit -45 in Cycle 73, the worst reading on record.  
**Target Duration:** 24 months (bridge to Baylight construction employment)  
**Lead Agency:** Oakland Economic & Workforce Development Department

-----

## II. The Problem

West Oakland is emptying.

|Indicator                               |Cycle 60|Cycle 73|Change|
|----------------------------------------|--------|--------|------|
|Migration Drift                         |-12     |-45     |-275% |
|Vacant Storefronts (7th Street corridor)|8       |23      |+188% |
|Small Business Closures (quarterly)     |6       |14      |+133% |
|Residential Vacancy Rate                |4.2%    |9.1%    |+117% |
|Median Rent (2BR)                       |$2,400  |$2,150  |-10%  |

The rent decline sounds like good news. It isn’t. It reflects collapsed demand — people leaving faster than prices can attract replacements. When landlords can’t fill units, they stop maintaining buildings. When storefronts stay dark, foot traffic disappears. The cycle accelerates.

Baylight District breaks ground in Year 3. That’s 18+ months away. West Oakland may not have 18 months.

## IV. Program Components

### Small Landlord Retention Grants

**Purpose:** Prevent small property owners (1-4 units) from selling to speculators or letting buildings deteriorate.

|Eligibility                                                                       |Grant Amount |
|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------|
|Owner-occupied 2-4 unit buildings                                                 |Up to $25,000|
|Non-owner-occupied 1-4 units (5+ year ownership)                                  |Up to $18,000|
|Required commitment: Maintain current tenants, no rent increases >3% for 24 months|—            |

**Allocation:** $8M  
**Target:** 400 properties

-----

### Commercial Rent Relief Program

**Purpose:** Keep small businesses in storefronts, prevent further vacancy spiral.

|Eligibility                                                 |Grant Amount                                 |
|------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------|
|Businesses operating 2+ years in West Oakland               |Up to $36,000 (covers 12 months partial rent)|
|New businesses opening in vacant storefronts                |Up to $24,000 (first-year rent subsidy)      |
|Required commitment: 24-month lease, local hiring preference|—                                            |

**Allocation:** $7M  
**Target:** 250 businesses

-----

### Longtime Resident Stability Grants

**Purpose:** Direct assistance to residents at risk of displacement.

|Eligibility                                                     |Grant Amount |
|----------------------------------------------------------------|-------------|
|Households at <80% AMI, 5+ year West Oakland residency          |Up to $8,000 |
|Seniors (65+) on fixed income, 10+ year residency               |Up to $12,000|
|Can be used for: back rent, moving prevention, emergency repairs|—            |

**Allocation:** $6M  
**Target:** 800 households

-----

### Storefront Activation Fund

**Purpose:** Reduce visible blight, create interim uses for vacant commercial space.

|Use                               |Funding                             |
|----------------------------------|------------------------------------|
|Pop-up business subsidies         |$50/sq ft for 6-month activations   |
|Community space conversions       |Up to $15,000 per storefront        |
|Public art installations          |Commissioned murals, window displays|
|Façade improvement matching grants|50% match up to $10,000             |

**Allocation:** $4M  
**Target:** 60 storefronts activated

-----

### Emergency Small Business Loans

**Purpose:** Bridge financing for businesses with viable plans but immediate cash flow gaps.

|Terms        |Details                           |
|-------------|----------------------------------|
|Loan Amount  |$10,000 - $75,000                 |
|Interest Rate|2% fixed                          |
|Repayment    |36-60 months, 6-month grace period|
|Collateral   |None required under $30,000       |

**Allocation:** $3M (revolving)  
**Target:** 50-75 loans

-----

## V. Administration

**Oversight:** West Oakland Stabilization Board (7 members)

- 2 City appointees (Economic Development, Housing)
- 2 Council appointees
- 3 West Oakland resident/business representatives

**Application Processing:**

- Small grants (<$15,000): 10-day turnaround
- Large grants (>$15,000): 21-day turnaround
- Loans: 30-day turnaround

**Compliance Monitoring:** Quarterly check-ins, clawback provisions for misuse

## VIII. Political Landscape

**Support:**

- Mayor Santana (West Oakland is core constituency)
- OPP council members
- West Oakland neighborhood associations
- Small business coalitions
- Anti-displacement advocacy groups

**Opposition:**

- CRC fiscal conservatives (“throwing money at a problem”)
- Budget hawks (reserve fund should be for genuine emergencies)
- Free market advocates (let the market correct)
- Some East Oakland representatives (why West Oakland first?)

**Key Tension:** The $12M reserve fund draw is the sticking point. Reserves exist for emergencies — but is a migration crisis an “emergency” in the budgetary sense? Santana’s team argues it qualifies. CRC will argue it’s policy, not emergency.

**Vote Count:** 6-3 required for reserve fund. Current projection: 5-4 OPP. One swing vote needed.

-----

## IX. Criticisms & Responses

|Criticism                                          |Response                                                                                                        |
|---------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|“This is a bailout for landlords”                  |Grants require tenant protection commitments. Landlords who raise rents or evict lose funding and face clawback.|
|“You can’t stop market forces”                     |We’re not trying to stop them. We’re trying to slow them long enough for Baylight employment to start.          |
|“$28M won’t fix West Oakland”                      |Correct. It buys 24 months. That’s the window we need.                                                          |
|“This should go to East Oakland / Fruitvale / etc.”|West Oakland’s numbers are worst. Triage means treating the most acute case first.                              |
|“The money will be wasted on fraud”                |Oversight board, clawback provisions, quarterly compliance. More accountability than most city programs.        |

-----

## X. Fund Narrative

West Oakland is not waiting for Baylight.

The moving trucks are running now. The storefronts are dark now. The “For Lease” signs multiply every cycle. You can promise people a stadium and 8,500 construction jobs, but if they’ve already moved to Phoenix by Year 3, the promise means nothing.

The Stabilization Fund is a bet — that $28M over 24 months can hold the line long enough for the big investments to land. It’s not elegant. It’s not a solution. It’s a tourniquet.

The alternative is watching West Oakland bleed out while everyone talks about what a great neighborhood it used to be.
