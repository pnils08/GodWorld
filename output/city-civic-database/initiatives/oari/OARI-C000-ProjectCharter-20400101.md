Public Safety Reform Program Status: Proposal Development — Council Introduction Pending

I. Program Overview
The Oakland Alternative Response Initiative (OARI) deploys trained civilian responders — not police — to mental health crises, wellness checks, non-violent disputes, and low-level quality-of-life calls. The program redirects 911 calls that don't require armed response to teams equipped to de-escalate, connect to services, and resolve situations without arrest.
This is Mayor Santana's most politically charged initiative — a direct application of her civil rights background to public safety policy.
Lead Agency: Oakland Fire Department (civilian division) Launch Target: Year 3, Q2 (pilot districts) Citywide Rollout: Year 4

II. The Case for Change
Oakland PD responds to calls that don't require police:
Call Type	Annual Volume	Armed Response Needed?
Mental Health Crisis	8,400	Rarely
Wellness Check	6,200	Rarely
Homeless/Encampment	9,800	No
Noise Complaint	5,100	No
Neighbor Dispute (non-violent)	4,300	Rarely
Intoxicated Person	3,900	Rarely
Trespassing (non-violent)	4,600	Rarely
Total Divertible Calls: ~42,000/year (approximately 28% of non-emergency dispatch)
Current Outcome: Officers spend hours on calls that don't match their training. Response times for actual crimes suffer. Encounters escalate unnecessarily. People who need help get handcuffs instead of services.

IV. Program Structure
Response Teams
CARE Teams (Crisis And Response Engagement)
	•	Composition: 1 licensed clinician + 1 EMT/community responder
	•	Vehicle: Unmarked SUV, medical equipment, no weapons
	•	Calls: Mental health crisis, suicide concern, emotional disturbance
	•	Staffing: 8 teams (16 FTE)
COMMUNITY Teams
	•	Composition: 2 trained community responders
	•	Vehicle: Marked city vehicle (non-police), no weapons
	•	Calls: Wellness checks, neighbor disputes, noise, trespassing
	•	Staffing: 12 teams (24 FTE)
OUTREACH Teams
	•	Composition: 1 social worker + 1 outreach specialist
	•	Vehicle: Van with supplies, resource materials
	•	Calls: Homeless/encampment, intoxicated person, service connection
	•	Staffing: 6 teams (12 FTE)
Dispatch Integration
	•	911 Call Triage: Dispatchers trained to identify divertible calls
	•	Screening Criteria: No weapon mentioned, no violence in progress, no active crime
	•	Backup Protocol: Police available if situation escalates (responder request)
	•	Response Time Target: 12 minutes (matches current OPD non-emergency average)

XI. The Political Fight
This will be the most contentious vote of Santana's term.
OPOA Position: The police union will frame this as "defunding" regardless of the actual budget mechanics. The $8.2M reallocation from OPD overtime — even though it doesn't cut officers or salaries — provides the talking point. Expect:
	•	Press conferences about "rising crime"
	•	Mailers to homeowners about "response times"
	•	Pressure on swing council votes
Santana's Counter:
	•	"This adds responders. It doesn't remove anyone."
	•	"Officers shouldn't spend their time on mental health calls. They're not trained for it."
	•	"Every hour we send a cop to a wellness check is an hour they're not stopping actual crime."
The Swing Vote: Likely Vega or another moderate. Whoever's up for re-election in a contested district feels the most pressure.
Vote Projection: 5-4 or 4-5. Genuinely uncertain.

XIII. Program Narrative
Avery Santana spent fifteen years suing police departments. She knows what happens when the wrong responder shows up to the wrong call. The wellness check that becomes a use-of-force incident. The mental health crisis that ends in arrest instead of treatment. The homeless man who needed a sandwich and got a citation.
The Alternative Response Initiative is her answer — not to defund anything, but to match the response to the call. Armed officers for armed situations. Trained civilians for everything else.
The police union will call it dangerous. The data from Denver, San Francisco, and Portland says otherwise. Zero use-of-force incidents. Lower re-contact rates. Higher resolution rates.
Oakland can keep sending cops to every call and wonder why nothing changes. Or it can try something that's already working in other cities.
Santana's betting her political capital on the second option.
